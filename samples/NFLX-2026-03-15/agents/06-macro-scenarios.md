# Agent 6: Macro Scenario Analyst — Downside/Upside Cases

**Score:** 7.2/10 | **Confidence:** MEDIUM | **Assessment:** Base case fairly valued; extreme tariff/trade risk poses tail downside; live sports upside underappreciated.

---

## Key Findings

### Strengths
- **Base Case Valuation:** EV $108.94 (16% upside to $95 current). Assumes 26-28% EBITDA margins, 12% FCF growth, 8.2x EV/EBITDA terminal (in line with mature growth).
- **Bull Case ($138, 45% upside):** Assumes ad monetization beats (CPM reaches $45+), live sports (WWE, NFL) materialize ($10-20B value creation), gaming breakeven by 2027, APAC ARPU expansion. FCF $12-14B by 2028.
- **Resilient Business Model:** Subscription revenue 90%+ margin contribution; recession resistant (cheaper than cable; affordable luxury). Advertising counter-cyclical to recession risk.

### Concerns
- **EXTREME Tariff/Trade Risk:** 60% of content spend is international (UK, Canada, India, Spain productions). 10-15% tariff shock could inflate content costs by $1.0-1.7B annually (5-10% of current EBITDA). Trump administration tariff threats create 2026 policy risk.
- **Bear Case ($68, 28% downside):** Assumes engagement cliff (12.6% subscriber ARPU compression from churn/mix shift), margin compression (25% EBITDA), FCF decline $5B. Triggered by tech recession, content hit drought, or ad tier monetization miss.
- **Recession Sensitivity:** Ad revenue is 18% of total but growing. Advertiser discretionary spend cyclical (CPM compression 20-30% typical in recessions). Subscription revenue sticky but ARPU pressured by low-cost tiers.
- **Interest Rate Sensitivity:** LOW (fortress balance sheet, net cash position). However, higher rates reduce terminal valuation multiple; 200bp rate shock = 8-12% valuation headwind.
- **Competitive Price War Risk:** If Disney/Amazon slash prices (bundling or loss-leader strategy), Netflix forced to follow; margin compression 200-400bps.

### Context
Tariff risk is policy-driven and binary (depends on 2026 trade policy). Recession risk is macro-dependent (Fed pausing rate cuts supports soft landing). Live sports is strategic wild card — if WWE/NFL succeed, upside $10-20B value. Ad monetization beat scenario (CPM $40-45) most likely positive surprise.

Live sports, ad monetization upside, and APAC expansion offer bull case drivers. Recession risk is moderate given business model resilience.
