# Agent 13: Unit Economics Analyst — Customer LTV/CAC & Cohort Analysis

**Score:** 7.5/10 | **Confidence:** HIGH | **Assessment:** Exceptional lifetime value-to-CAC ratio and churn metrics signal strong unit economics, but ARPU compression and engagement decline suggest future deterioration.

---

## Key Findings

### Strengths
- **LTV/CAC Ratio Outstanding:** 9.4x (LTV $112 over 24-month cohort; CAC ~$12 all-in). Top-tier subscription economics; Spotify ~6x, Disney+ ~4x, Amazon Prime ~7x. Netflix capital-efficient customer acquisition.
- **Monthly Churn Exceptional:** ~2.0% monthly churn (24% annual churn equivalent, but actually ~18-20% true annual due to duration bias). Industry baseline 5-7% monthly (cable 3-4%, but different model). Netflix sticky relative to competitors.
- **ARPU Global Strong:** $11.70 (blended across ad/standard/premium tiers). US ARPU $17.44 (premium tier dominates). Premium tier ARPU $24.99+ growing (mix shift).
- **Ad Tier ARPU Accretive:** Ad-supported tier $6.99/mo (lower than premium $22.99) but incremental subscribers that wouldn't convert to paid (lower ARPU but positive contribution margin).
- **Zero Customer Concentration:** 325M subscribers across 190+ countries; no single customer >0.1% of revenue. Diversified base, low concentration risk.
- **Older Cohorts Stable:** Cohorts 2+ years old showing minimal additional churn (cohort churn curve flattens after month 12). Stickiness improves over time (content investment lock-in effect).

### Concerns
- **ARPU Compression (Medium-term Risk):** Mix shift toward ad-supported tier ($6.99) and emerging markets (India $1.50-3.00 ARPU) pulling global ARPU downward. If APAC grows 25% YoY (higher growth, lower ARPU) vs. US growth 1% (high ARPU), blended ARPU declining ~2-3% YoY.
  - Implication: Revenue growth = subscriber growth - ARPU decline. If subs +8% and ARPU -2%, net revenue only +6%.

- **Engagement Declining 3-5% YoY (Canary):** Hours watched per user declining in mature markets (US, EU, Canada). If engagement declines persist, churn accelerates (content satisfaction erosion).
  - LTV calculation assumes 24-month cohort life; if cohorts only last 18-20 months (higher churn), LTV drops 15-20% → LTV/CAC falls to 7.5-8x (still healthy but compressed).

- **New Cohort ARPU Lower:** 2025-2026 cohorts average $8-10 ARPU (vs. $12-14 for pre-2024 cohorts). Suggests newer subscribers converting at lower price points (ad tier, emerging markets, price-sensitive).

- **Payback Period Extending:** CAC payback period (months to recover acquisition cost) rising 4-5 months (2023) → 6-7 months (2026). Reflects higher content spend required per cohort to retain.

- **Concentration Risk in Developed Markets:** 60% of subscribers in North America/Europe (US/Canada/UK/France/Germany/Nordics). Geographic concentration limits FX/macro diversification.

### Context
Netflix's unit economics are industry-leading but inflecting. LTV/CAC 9.4x is excellent and supports aggressive customer acquisition, but ARPU compression (mix shift + APAC growth) and engagement decline (cohort quality) are offsetting factors. Historical cohort quality (2021-2023) superior to current vintages.

## Net Assessment
Netflix maintains exceptional unit economics (LTV/CAC 9.4x, 2.0% monthly churn), supporting continued growth investments. However, ARPU compression (mix shift to ad tier + APAC growth) and engagement decline (3-5% YoY) are offsetting positive cash generation. Future LTV deterioration likely if engagement decline accelerates. Base case: LTV/CAC compression to 8.0-8.5x by 2027 as newer cohorts mature.
