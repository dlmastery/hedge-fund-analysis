## Key Findings

### Strengths
- **RSI Extreme Oversold:** RSI 23.5 (below 30 = oversold; below 20 = panic). Historical data shows RSI <25 typically precedes 5-15% bounces within 2-4 weeks.
- **Institutional Buying Support:** Institutional ownership 80.93% (Vanguard, BlackRock, Capital Group top 3 holders with $89B+ combined). Large endowments and pensions unlikely to abandon at lows.
- **Short Squeeze Potential:** Short interest 2.8% of float (~$11B notional). Low by internet stock standards but concentrated among hedge funds; quick bounce above $100 could force shorts to cover.
- **Put/Call Skew Contrarian:** Put/call ratio 1.3x elevated (vs. 0.8x avg) = market pricing downside risk. Contrarian indicator: excessive hedging often marks capitulation.

### Concerns
- **Death Cross Formation:** 50-day MA below 200-day MA — negative momentum signal. Historically precedes further weakness 60% of the time (3-6 month duration).
- **Crowd Positioning Risk:** 81% bullish sell-side ratings despite 15% ytd drawdown = consensus reversal risk. "Everyone bullish" contradicts the 23.5 RSI — conflicting signals.
- **Hedge Fund Liquidation Risk:** Reduced positioning detected in January 2026 hedge fund surveys. If macro deteriorates (rate shock), forced selling could cascade.
- **Negative Divergence:** RSI 23.5 but stock near 52-week lows; breadth indicators weakening. Bounce could be dead-cat rather than reversal.

### Context
Technical metrics are useful but non-predictive on individual timescales. RSI extremes do correlate with reversals, but Netflix's fundamental headwinds (insider selling, margin pressure) could override technical support. Dead cat bounces common in declining growth narratives.
