# Agent 3: Governance Forensics Analyst — Corporate Governance

**Score:** 7.0/10 | **Confidence:** MEDIUM | **Assessment:** Generally strong governance, but insider selling panic and co-CEO ambiguity cloud near-term confidence.

---

## Key Findings

### Strengths
- **Board Independence:** 10 of 11 directors independent (91% independence). No interlocks with other media companies. Audit/comp/nom committees fully independent.
- **No Entrenchment Mechanisms:** No poison pill. No dual-class shares. One share = one vote. Activist-friendly governance structure.
- **Clean Capital Allocation:** $15B buyback authorization with $6.2B executed in 2025. Share repurchases at reasonable valuations (avg $75-92). No empire-building M&A.
- **Executive Compensation Structure:** Total comp ~$300M across top 5; 60% variable tied to revenue/margin metrics. Not excessive by media standards.

### Concerns
- **MASSIVE Insider Selling (RED FLAG):** $287M net insider sales in past 90 days with ZERO insider buying. Pattern: Sarandos, Peters, and board members liquidating simultaneously. Classic pre-decline signal or portfolio rebalancing, but concentrated timing suggests knowledge of near-term headwinds.
- **Co-CEO Model Ambiguity:** Sarandos (content) + Peters (product/tech) co-lead without clear hierarchy. Hastings as Executive Chairman still influential but unclear. Succession plan opaque — no clear #2 or defined pathway.
- **DOJ Antitrust Review:** Failed attempt to acquire Warner Bros. Discovery (2024) triggered antitrust scrutiny. Potential restrictions on future M&A or joint ventures could constrain strategic optionality.
- **No Recent Board Refreshes:** Several directors 6+ year tenured; stale perspectives on AI, gaming, live sports transformation.

### Context
Netflix historically had governance controversies (co-founder, CEO transitions) but has reformed significantly. Insider selling on this scale (vs. historical patterns of buy-and-hold founders) is notable inflection. Co-CEO models work (Apple had Jony Ive + Cook parallel), but clarity on #1 decision-maker matters.

Netflix's governance structure is strong and shareholder-friendly, but the $287M insider selling without offsetting buying is a significant red flag suggesting management knowledge of upcoming challenges. The co-CEO model and unclear succession plan create organizational risk if near-term execution falters. DOJ antitrust scrutiny adds regulatory drag.
