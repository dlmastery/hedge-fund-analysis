- Base case: Fleet deployment 50K–100K vehicles by 2029, $25–30/mile economics, $500M–2B annual revenue. Risk-adjusted: $20–40B (assuming 20% prob of commercial fleet viability and profitability).

- Trigger events: FSD L4 approval with legal liability framework, fleet profitability demonstration (2028), insurance/regulatory green light.

**Optimus (Humanoid Robot) TAM ($200B+ possible, 10% probability):**
- Base case: Labor substitution, logistics, manufacturing. 10M+ robots deployed by 2035 at $50K/unit = $500B TAM. Tesla's share 20–40% plausible = $100–200B TAM. Risk-adjusted: $10–30B (assuming 10% prob and long-tail uncertainty).
- Extreme case: If general-purpose robotics achieves AGI-adjacent capabilities, TAM could reach $1T+ (disruption of 20–30% of human labor).
- Trigger events: Optimus prototype commercial deployment (2027–2028), cost curve inflection below $25K/unit, labor shortage dynamics accelerating.

**Energy/Grid Services ($50B by 2030, 60% probability):**
- Base case: Energy storage 150+ GWh capacity, grid services, virtual power plant software. $15–20B revenue by 2030, 25% operating margin = $3.75–5B operating income. Risk-adjusted: $30–50B (assuming 60% prob, highest confidence of all optionality).
- Trigger events: IRA tax credits extended, utility contract wins >100K/yr, Tesla energy software (Autobidder) commercialized to third-party systems.

**Insurance TAM ($10B, 50% probability):**
- Base case: Tesla Insurance currently $100–150M revenue. Expansion to captive insurance for all Tesla owners ($1,800–2,000/yr × 2M owners = $3.6–4B TAM). Risk-adjusted: $5–8B (assuming 50% prob).
- Trigger events: Full autonomous capabilities (lower claims frequency), regulatory approval for national expansion, loss ratio discipline <85%.
