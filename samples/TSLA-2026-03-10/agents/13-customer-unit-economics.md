## Key Findings

### Average Selling Price (ASP) Compression

**Historical Trend:**
- 2022: $52,000 average selling price (peak, favorable mix of Model S/X premium products)
- 2023: $46,500 (price cuts initiated)
- 2024: $44,300 (aggressive pricing to maintain volume during slowdown)
- 2025: $43,200 (current, largely stabilized)
- 2026E: $44,500 (modest recovery expected from Cybertruck mix + new Model 3/Y refresh)

**Structural Drivers:**
- Demand elasticity at work: Each $1,000 price cut yields ~2–3% unit volume increase (price-elastic demand segment). Tesla managing volume via aggressive pricing rather than demand stimulation (vs. legacy OEMs heavy advertising spend).
- Product mix shift: Model 3/Y (lower ASP $42–45K) now 85% of volumes, up from 70% in 2022. Model S/X (ASP $80K+) declining in volume. This mix shift alone accounts for ~$3K ASP decline.
- Cybertruck entering mix (ASP $55K–75K) offset by volume still ramping (200K units 2025, growing). By 2027, Cybertruck 15–20% of volume could add $1–2K to blended ASP.

**Forward Outlook:** ASP stabilization at $44–46K expected 2026–2027 as Cybertruck mix increases and price-driven volume exhaustion ceiling reached. Further ASP declines unlikely unless market share loss accelerates (low probability).

### Gross Margin Compression & Recovery Path

**Historical Trend:**
- 2022: 25.1% gross margin (peak, before price cuts)
- 2023: 21.4% (price cut impact)
- 2024: 18.5% (continued margin pressure)
- 2025: 18.2% (stabilizing, improving slightly vs. mid-year 2024)
- 2026E: 19.2% (modest recovery expected from 4680 ramp, manufacturing efficiency)
- 2027E: 20.5% (recovery trajectory continued)

**Margin Recovery Drivers (2026–2027):**
1. **4680 cell cost reduction:** Panasonic ramp + Tesla in-house production reducing battery pack cost $2–3K per unit. At ~25% of vehicle cost, this represents 1.5–2% gross margin gain.
2. **Manufacturing efficiency:** Berlin, Austin gigafactories reaching nameplate capacity and improving yield rates. Typical factory learning curve: -3% to -5% cost reduction in years 3–5 of operation. Both factories in years 2–3 of operation in 2026–2027.
3. **Regulatory credit decline, offsetting positive:** Credits declining from $1.8B to $1.2B annually represents 150 basis points margin headwind. Offset by mix improvement + manufacturing gains = net positive 100 bps by 2027.
4. **FSD attach growth:** FSD attach increasing from 24% to estimated 30–35% by 2027. Each FSD unit adds $10–12K to vehicle lifetime gross profit (assuming $500/yr subscription × 15 years). FSD attach alone could add 50–75 bps to vehicle gross margin.

**Peer Benchmark:** Legacy OEMs targeting 18–22% vehicle gross margin as EV transition progresses. Tesla's 18.2% (2025) is in-line, and 20%+ (2027E) would be 200+ bps above legacy OEMs. Management guidance for "20%+ by 2027" appears credible.

### Lifetime Value (LTV) Model

**Vehicle LTV (Core):**
- Base vehicle revenue: ASP $43K, gross margin 18.2% = $7.8K gross profit per vehicle
- Vehicle lifespan: 5–6 years before trade-in or continued ownership
- Annual depreciation: Year 1 (-15%), Years 2–5 (-3–5% annually)
- **Vehicle gross profit contribution: $7.8K per sale**

**FSD Subscription LTV:**
- FSD attach rate: 24% of new vehicles, growing to 30–35% by 2027
- Price: $99/month (raised from $79 in 2024)
- Assume 8–10 year ownership period (typical: 5–6 active subscription, 2–4 years potential continuation if enabled)
- **FSD subscription: $99/mo × 100 months = $9,900 gross revenue, ~80% margin = $7,920 lifetime gross profit**
- **Applied to new vehicles: 24% attach × $7,920 = $1,901 incremental gross profit per vehicle cohort**

**Supercharger Network Revenue LTV:**
- Average owner: 200+ Supercharger sessions over vehicle lifetime
- Revenue per session: $3–4 (varies by kWh pricing)
- **Supercharger LTV: 200 sessions × $3.50 = $700 gross revenue, 60% margin = $420 incremental gross profit**

**Insurance LTV:**
- Tesla Insurance: ~18% of owners adopt (target 30% by 2027)
- Premium: $1,800/year
- Loss ratio: 35–40% (vs. 65–70% industry, Tesla data advantage helps)
- Assumption: 6-year ownership period
- **Insurance LTV: 18% adopt × $1,800/yr × 6 yrs × 45% margin = $874 per vehicle cohort gross profit**

**Other Services (parts, installation, maintenance):**
- $400/year average across 5-year ownership = $2,000 gross revenue
- **Services margin: 20% = $400 gross profit per vehicle**

**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost):** Effectively $0. Tesla has no advertising budget for consumer vehicles. Word-of-mouth, referral program, brand equity drive acquisition. This is ~2–3% of traditional automotive CAC ($500–1,500 typical).

**LTV/CAC Ratio:** ∞ (infinite). No traditional customer acquisition cost means all vehicle gross profit is net. This is unique advantage vs. Ford, GM, etc. paying $800–2,000 per vehicle in advertising/dealer incentives.

## Net Assessment

Customer unit economics are **transitioning from compression phase (2022–2025) to recovery phase (2026–2027).** The gross margin trough of 18–18.2% appears near, with recovery to 20%+ credible by 2027 based on (1) 4680 battery ramp, (2) manufacturing efficiency, (3) FSD attach growth, (4) mixed positive from Cybertruck. Vehicle LTV is improving modestly (+4–10% CAGR 2026–2027) despite ASP compression, driven by higher-margin recurring revenue (FSD, insurance, Supercharging). The lack of CAC is strategic moat vs. legacy OEMs, enabling Tesla to reinvest margin gains into R&D and capex rather than customer acquisition. **Base case: LTV stabilizes at $10.5–11.5K, supporting 20%+ vehicle gross margin by 2027.** Downside scenario (30% probability): FSD monetization delays, insurance adoption slower, margin recovery stalls = LTV could remain at $10K. Upside scenario (20% probability): FSD L4 approval enables licensing revenue + robotaxi optionality = LTV exceeds $12K.
