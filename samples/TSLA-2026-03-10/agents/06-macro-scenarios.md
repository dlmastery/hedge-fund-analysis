## Key Findings

### Structural Tailwinds
- **IRA credits extended through 2032 ($7,500/vehicle):** US government codified EV subsidies for next 7 years. Removes regulatory repeal risk that plagued 2021–2024 period. For Tesla-qualified vehicles, margin support of $7,500/unit guaranteed, $3,750 battery domestic content bonus if applicable. This alone provides $3.6B annual revenue support.
- **Global EV penetration inflection (12%→18% by 2026):** IEA and BloombergNEF consensus that 2026 marks inflection from niche to mass-market. Cumulative penetration crossing 15–18% drives network effects (charging availability, service density, insurance products maturity). Tesla positioned as market leader during inflection.
- **AI infrastructure capex boom benefiting energy storage:** $600B+ global AI/compute capex cycle creating electricity demand surge. Data centers prioritizing reliable power, backup systems, grid stability products. Energy storage TAM expanding from $50B to $150B+ by 2030. Tesla's 100+ GWh capacity platform perfectly positioned.
- **Battery cost curve inflection downward:** Battery pack costs declined to $87/kWh (2025) from $102/kWh (2023), -15% YoY. Trajectory toward $75/kWh by 2027 (Bloomberg NEF consensus) improves all vehicle ASP/margin calculus. Makes sub-$30K vehicle economically viable by 2027–2028.

### Cyclical Headwinds
- **Fed funds rate at 4.25% (elevated vs pre-2022):** Credit availability tight for lower-income EV buyers. Monthly payment sensitivity high in sub-$35K segment. Headwind for BYD/Seagull mass-market expansion but less relevant for Tesla positioning (premium/mid-premium buyers).
- **Tariff uncertainty (25% China EV tariff threat):** Biden/Trump tariff regimes create supply chain ambiguity. Tesla China exports (0 units currently due to Shanghai geopolitical risk) not directly impacted, but competitiveness of imported Chinese EVs changes. Could support US pricing if China EVs face 25% tariffs.
- **EV subsidy political risk (2026–2028 election cycle):** GOP could reduce/repeal IRA credits post-2026 if they gain legislative control. Would reduce margin support $1–2B annually but not fundamentally change EV trend. Medium-term risk, not base case.
- **Consumer discretionary weakening:** Q4 2025 consumer confidence down 8% QoQ. Luxury/premium purchases sensitive. Tesla order backlog declining Q3→Q4 2025, though attributed to price cuts driving velocity over margin. Demand destruction not evident, but sentiment fragile.
- **Oil price at $65/bbl (reducing EV urgency):** Fuel cost advantage of EVs diminished. TCO (total cost of ownership) gap narrows from $8,000 to $4,500 over 5-year hold period. Reduces "must have" motivation but not game-changing for decision-making in mature EV markets.

### Scenario Modeling Framework

**Calibration Rules:**
1. Base case = continuation of trends observable in 2024–2025 data
2. Bull case = upper quartile of distribution, not absurd tail
3. Bear case = lower quartile, plausible but low probability (<20%)
4. Sanity floor = profitable companies value ≥0.5x revenue
5. Consensus anchor = median analyst FV ±15% as reality check

**Base Case ($430–470):** Margin stabilizes at 18–19%, unit growth 4–6% CAGR, energy revenue $18–20B by 2027, FSD attach reaches 30%, macro neutral (rates stable, trade stable). Probability 60%.

**Bull Case ($550–700):** Margin recovers to 22%+, unit growth accelerates to 8–12% (mix shift toward Cybertruck, refreshed Model 3/Y), energy $25–30B revenue, FSD L4 approval and licensing generates $5–10B incremental, macro supportive (rates down, AI infrastructure boom). Probability 25%.

**Bear Case ($250–350):** Recession triggered by Fed policy error, tariffs escalate to 40%+ on China, China market share collapses to 5–8%, margin compressed to 10%, FSD regulatory setback, EV demand destruction. Probability 15%.
