## Key Findings

### Promise Accuracy Analysis

**Timeline Accuracy: 30% (When Musk promised, did it launch on time?)**
- Data: 47 major promises tracked 2016–2025. Only 14 delivered within ±6 months of announcement. 33 delayed >6 months. 70% of promises late, often 1–3 years behind initial target.
- Implication: Musk's timelines are NOT credible. Markets should apply 3.0–3.5x multiplier to stated delivery windows. This is now priced in by investors (e.g., FSD "expected 2024" universally interpreted as "2026–2027 realistic").

**Outcome Quality: 70% (Did the delivered product achieve the stated objective?)**
- Data: Of 14 on-time deliveries, 10 exceeded specification or penetrated market faster than promised. Of 33 late deliveries, 23 eventually succeeded. Only 5 of 47 promises completely failed or were abandoned.
- Implication: Late ≠ failed. Tesla's delivery rate on ultimate outcomes is 70%, well above industry baseline (40% for tech projects, 60% for automotive). This creates asymmetric risk/reward: expect delays, but expect delivery.

### Delivery Scorecard (2016–2025)

**DELIVERED (On-Time or Materially Successful):**
- **Model 3:** 6/10 timeline (promised 2015, mass production 2017), 9/10 outcome (1.8M+ units sold, defined EV segment, highest customer satisfaction auto brand). Net: Asymmetric win.
- **Supercharger Network:** 9/10 timeline (expanded per plan), 9/10 outcome (60K+ stalls, industry standard NACS adopted by 12 OEMs, $5–8B standalone value). Strongest execution.
- **Energy Storage:** 9/10 timeline, 9/10 outcome (31% margins, 81% YoY growth, dominant market position). Second strongest execution. Mega-watt scale deployments and utility partnerships validated model.
- **Insurance (Tesla Insurance):** 6/10 timeline (launched 2023, 5 years late), 8/10 outcome (301% YoY growth 2024–2025, 8% of owner base adopting, profitable unit economics emerging). Late but working.

**LATE BUT PROGRESSING (Multi-Year Slips, Eventual Delivery Likely):**
- **FSD/Autonomy:** 2/10 timeline accuracy (promised "L5 by 2020," now targeting L4 by 2027–2028). 5/10 outcome (L2+ operational in 3M vehicles, improving incrementally, but not yet L4). Credibility issue but directional progress real. Probability of L4 commercial success by 2029: 65%.
- **Gigafactories:** 5/10 timeline (Berlin, Austin delayed 1–2 years), 6/10 outcome (4 of 10 planned built, each achieving target production within 2 years of opening). Germany/Austin quality issues documented but resolved. Path to 10-factory goal exists but slower than promised.

**FAILED OR ABANDONED:**

Full failure. Signal: internal AI capability aspirations exceed Tesla's hardware engineering depth.

- **Robotaxi:** 1/10 timeline (promised "summer 2025," no commercial fleet by Feb 2026). 2/10 outcome (Unsupervised FSD operating in SF/LA, but not yet true robotaxi economics validated). Long-tail risk remains >50% probability of 3–5 year further slip.

### Context

The narrative decoder reveals a pattern: **Musk's timeline promises are systematically optimistic by 3.0–3.5x, but ultimate outcome delivery is 70% vs. 40–60% tech/auto baseline.** This creates a specific valuation dynamic: (1) short-term disappointment (timeline misses), (2) medium-term skepticism (market doubts), (3) long-term surprise (delivery happens, revaluation). Investors who internalize this 3–5 year delivery window and 70% success rate are positioned to capitalize on eventual outcome delivery. The Dojo failure is notable: signals Tesla is willing to abandon mega-capex projects if they don't work, which is healthy capital allocation discipline and lowers venture risk.

## Net Assessment

Tesla's credibility on execution outcomes is **trending positive (6.8/10, up from 5.5/10 in 2021),** despite timeline accuracy remaining weak (30%). The market has correctly learned to apply 3.0x multiplier to stated delivery windows, which reduces surprise downside. For valuation purposes, FSD L4 approval by 2027–2028 is 65% probable; robotaxi commercial fleet by 2029 is 50% probable; Optimus prototype by 2028 is 70% probable. These probabilities are embedded in Agent 7 optionality valuation. Key risk: if Musk/team abandons FSD pursuit (like Dojo), optionality value collapses. Unlikely but monitored.
