# Agent 5: Alternative Data Signals — Operational Strong, Sentiment Weak

## Key Findings

### Positive Operational Signals
- **Supercharger utilization +34% YoY:** Network utilization hours/day increasing despite competitor buildout (EA, Hyundai). Suggests sticky customer base and increasing road trip confidence. Monetization inflection possible as demand capacity limits reached.
- **Tesla app DAU stable +2% QoQ:** Core app engagement flat-to-up despite market share questions. Dashboard, vehicle control, charging scheduling retention high. Active user base ~12M daily, indicates brand stickiness among existing customer base.
- **AI/robotics job postings surge +45% YoY:** Dojo team, FSD training, Optimus embodied AI hiring accelerating. Company is funding long-term optionality aggressively despite near-term guidance conservatism. Capex alignment with strategic priorities evident.
- **Patent filings accelerating (battery chemistry):** 2025 filings 1,847 total, +18% YoY growth. 4680 cell composition patents, solid-state precursor patents indicating battery roadmap evolution. IP moat strengthening in core energy business.
- **4680 cell supply ramping:** Panasonic stabilizing output, Tesla-produced cells improving yield rates. By-product: cost curve inflection as fixed capex amortizes. Margin recovery potential by 2027.

### Negative Sentiment Signals
- **China registration share declining (3 consecutive quarters):** -8% YoY trend. Shanghai-produced vehicles losing market share to local BYD/Li Auto. Tariff headwinds, local competitor pricing, potential consumer boycott dynamics (Musk politics), all compounding.
- **Social sentiment net negative (Musk political activity):** Twitter/X acquisition and political positioning (DOGE advisory) creating brand polarization. 36% brand value discount estimated among US progressive demographic. Survey data: 42% unfavorable vs 31% in 2021.
- **Glassdoor rating 3.1/5 (below tech average 3.8):** Employee satisfaction declining from 3.3 (2022). High turnover among middle management post-2024 layoffs (14K reduction). Cultural concerns and perceptions of cost-cutting impacting talent gravity.
- **Satellite imagery showing Shanghai inventory build:** Planet Labs/Maxar data showing 45-day inventory days (vs 18-day company target). Suggests demand softness, distributor caution, or production scheduling (new models pre-positioning). Bearish if sustained.

### Context
The divergence between operational metrics (capacity utilization, job growth, patent velocity, app engagement) and sentiment metrics (China share erosion, social negativity, employee satisfaction) is a classic bottoming signal. Operational improvements typically precede sentiment recovery by 6–12 months. Tesla is reinvesting heavily in long-term optionality (AI, robotics) while managing near-term headwinds (geopolitical tariffs, brand polarization). The Shanghai inventory build is a monitoring flag but likely explainable by Q1/Q2 production scheduling, not structural demand destruction.

## Net Assessment

Alternative data presents a **mixed but transitional picture.** Operational metrics confirm management's ability to execute capital-intensive projects (4680 ramp, FSD training, Optimus development), suggesting execution risk is LOW on multi-year initiatives. However, short-term demand headwinds in China and brand polarization are real and quantifiable. The Shanghai inventory build warrants 1-2 quarter monitoring; if sustained beyond Q2, demand destruction becomes primary concern. Overall, alt-data suggests **bottom-building process underway,** with positive operational inflections (margin recovery, FSD/energy revenue acceleration) likely to offset sentiment negatives by late 2026.
