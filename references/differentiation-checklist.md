# Differentiation Checklist: What Makes This Institutional-Grade

Use this checklist to verify the final report is genuinely differentiated from
retail research products. Each item below represents something that is typically
NOT found in products you can buy from Morningstar, Seeking Alpha, or sell-side research.

## vs. Morningstar ($199-$499/year)
Morningstar provides: moat rating, fair value estimate, star rating, basic financials.
Our report adds:

- [ ] Forensic accounting with calculated Beneish M-Score and Sloan Accrual Ratio
- [ ] Working capital manipulation detection (DSO/DIO/DPO trend analysis)
- [ ] Off-balance-sheet exposure quantification
- [ ] Stock-based comp as hidden dilution analysis
- [ ] Adjusted EBITDA add-back legitimacy assessment
- [ ] Reverse DCF showing market-implied assumptions
- [ ] Multi-scenario probability-weighted valuation (not just one fair value)
- [ ] Kill criteria that are specific and measurable

## vs. ALL Retail & Sell-Side Research (the "Secret Sauce" layer)
These dimensions exist in hedge fund internal reports but are virtually NEVER
available in any purchasable research product:

- [ ] **Sum-of-the-parts with optionality pricing** — decomposing a 300x P/E into
  what pays for current business vs. probability-weighted future business lines
- [ ] **S-curve positioning analysis** — where is each business segment on its adoption curve,
  with historical penetration rate analogues
- [ ] **TAM bridge analysis** — TAM → SAM → share → revenue → margin → NPV for each future line
- [ ] **Execution milestone credibility tracker** — promise vs. delivery ledger over 3+ years
- [ ] **Real options framework** — treating unproven business lines as options with strike price,
  time to expiry, and volatility
- [ ] **Network effects / flywheel mapping** — explicit node-by-node quantification of feedback loops
- [ ] **Implied expectations stress test** — reverse-engineering what EACH business line must
  achieve at the current price (not just aggregate reverse DCF)
- [ ] **Second-order effects mapping** — dependency trees showing how success in one line
  creates options in others
- [ ] **Earnings call language evolution** — tracking confidence markers, hedging language,
  and buzzword shifts across 4-8 quarters
- [ ] **Promise tracking ledger** — comprehensive table of management promises, deadlines,
  outcomes, and excuse patterns
- [ ] **Goal post movement detection** — specific instances of changed KPIs, dropped metrics,
  or redefined success criteria
- [ ] **Guidance sandbagging score** — statistical analysis of beat rate, magnitude, and trend
- [ ] **Capital allocation: words vs. deeds** — comparing stated priorities to actual cash flows
- [ ] **Competitive narrative arc tracking** — how management's language about competitors
  shifts from dismissive → acknowledging → defensive
- [ ] **Comparable optionality pricing** — finding 2-3 companies similarly priced for
  future optionality and what happened to them

## The Tesla Test (the real differentiation bar)
For a stock like TSLA at 360x P/E, a retail report says "overvalued" or "growth stock."
Our report should answer:
- Exactly what % of the market cap is paying for the car business vs. FSD vs. robotaxi
  vs. energy vs. Optimus vs. insurance vs. AI compute
- Where each of those business lines sits on its adoption S-curve
- What penetration rate each line needs to achieve for the current price to be justified
- Whether management has actually delivered on the specific milestones they promised
  for each of these lines
- How their language about each initiative has shifted over the last 2 years
- 2-3 historical companies that were priced similarly for optionality and what happened
- Whether the flywheel effects between business lines justify a platform premium
- Probability-weighted valuation that accounts for optionality, not just DCF

## The Ultimate Test
A PM at a multi-billion dollar fund reading this report should think:
"This covers dimensions I'd normally need 5 different analysts to produce,
it explains the FORWARD story not just the backward data, and it flagged
at least 3-5 things I hadn't considered."

If the report doesn't pass this test, it needs more depth.
